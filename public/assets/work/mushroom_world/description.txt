I have loved mushrooms since I was a child—they feel both adorable and quietly alive, almost like they form their own gentle society. To me, they are calm, humble, soft, and comforting.

This installation tells the story of a mushroom forest that was once part of the ocean. Over time, due to tectonic movement, a whale skull remained, surrounded by growing mushrooms.
Spirits living within the forest gather here to learn, survive, and reproduce, protecting the forest across generations.
